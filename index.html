I
Chapter One · The Northern Opener

Begin where the sun won't set

The trip opens far to the north — a different planet before the Italian heat, and a nine-year-old's dream. Two ways to do it, planned in full below. The numbers and the flight times point hard to the first.

★ Our pick

Reykjavík & the Ring of Fire

A five-hour red-eye from Boston drops you into a land of geysers, black-sand beaches, and waterfalls you can walk behind — beneath a sky that glows for twenty-one hours a day. Three or four days here, then one Icelandair hop carries you straight on to Milan, no second ticket needed.

Boarding
BOS ✈ KEF
Icelandair FI630 · dep 8:50 PM · ~5h15
One ticket through to Milan · free Iceland stopover

Four days in Iceland

Day 1 · Arrival

Soft landing & the Blue Lagoon

Off the overnight flight and into the slow lane. Wander Reykjavík's Rainbow Street up to Hallgrímskirkja, then melt into the milky-warm Blue Lagoon — right on the airport road, free for kids. Stay up to local bedtime to beat the jet lag.

Don't missA silica mud-mask float.
TipSwimsuit in the carry-on; leave conditioner in your hair.
Day 2 · The Golden Circle

Geysers & a continental rift

Iceland's greatest hits in one loop: walk between two tectonic plates at Þingvellir, watch Strokkur fire skyward every few minutes, and stand over the thundering two-tier Gullfoss. Lunch among the vines at the Friðheimar tomato greenhouse.

Don't missStrokkur erupting.
TipSelf-drive beats the bus — and reserve Friðheimar ahead.
Day 3 · The South Coast

Waterfall country & black sand

Walk the path behind Seljalandsfoss, feel the spray off mighty Skógafoss, then meet the basalt columns and sea stacks of Reynisfjara's black-sand beach near Vík.

Don't missWalking behind the falling water.
Tip⚠️ Reynisfjara's sneaker waves are deadly — keep well back from the surf.
Day 4 · Flex day

Out on the water

Our pick for a nine-year-old: a whale-watching cruise from Reykjavík's old harbour — minke whales, dolphins, and summer puffins — then the water-slides at Laugardalslaug pool or the indoor ice cave at Perlan.

Don't missA humpback breach in Faxaflói Bay.
TipDress warmer than you'd think; pack motion-sickness tablets.

or, the storybook alternative

Copenhagen, the easy city

The gentler-in-spirit option: a flat, bike-friendly capital of candy-coloured canals and the world's loveliest old amusement park, warmer than Iceland and walkable end to end — then a short hop down to Pisa, closest to the coast.

Boarding
BOS ✈ CPH
SAS SK928 · dep 5:40 PM · ~7h25
Then CPH → Pisa · 1.5h train to Monterosso
⚠️ Flight reality: the only nonstop Boston→Copenhagen leaves 5:40 PM — tight to catch after a 1 PM finish in Simsbury — and runs $1,000+ per person for June 25. It's the pricier, trickier opener (see The Numbers).

Four days in Copenhagen

Day 1 · Arrival

Harbour, canals & Tivoli lights

Ease in along candy-coloured Nyhavn, drift the canals by boat past the Little Mermaid and Amalienborg, then end at Tivoli Gardens as it lights up at dusk.

Don't missTivoli glowing at twilight.
TipGet the unlimited-ride wristband if your kid goes hard.
Day 2 · Royal city

Crown jewels by bike

Copenhagen is flat and bike-mad. Pedal to Rosenborg Castle for the Danish Crown Jewels, run loose in the King's Garden, then coast down Strøget to the Lego flagship.

Don't missThe Crown Jewels treasury.
TipRide on the right and signal — locals enforce lane etiquette.
Day 3 · Day trip

Vikings or Hamlet

Your pick: row a reconstructed longship at Roskilde's Viking Ship Museum (our nod for a nine-year-old), or explore the torch-lit casemates of Kronborg — Hamlet's castle.

Don't missRowing a Viking longboat (summer only).
TipBoth are easy DIY by train — no tour needed.

II
Chapter Two · The Ligurian Coast · Jul 1–5

Five villages, one little train

The warm heart of the trip, and the one fixed point on the calendar. Monterosso al Mare — the only Cinque Terre village with a real sandy beach — is home base for four days of swimming, focaccia, and pesto where it was invented.

The five villages are stitched into the cliffs and linked by the Cinque Terre Express, a regional train that pops between tunnels every few minutes. Hop them at will, ride a boat past the headlands at golden hour, and take the gentle coastal paths between swims.

  • Village-hop — Riomaggiore, Manarola, Corniglia, Vernazza, Monterosso
  • Beach days — Monterosso's sand, sun, and seaside cafés
  • By boat — the whole coast from the water at sunset
Fixed datesHotel booked4 nights
À table · Liguria
  • Trofie al pesto — hand-rolled pasta twists with basil pesto. dal Billy, Manarola
  • Focaccia di Recco — molten-cheese-filled thin focaccia
  • Acciughe di Monterosso — prized anchovies, raw or fried
  • Farinata — crisp baked chickpea pancake
  • Frittura mista — a paper cone of fried seafood
III
Chapter Three · The Lakes I · Jul 5–8

Lake Como, impossibly elegant

Glacier-carved, villa-lined, and gorgeous from every angle. From a base in Varenna you ferry between the lake's prettiest towns, wander garden terraces, and swim off warm stone — somewhere brand new to all three of you.

Make Varenna your base — small, romantic, and on the train line — then let the ferries do the work. Bellagio and Menaggio are minutes across the water, and a boat ride away sits one of cinema's most famous villas.

  • Ferry-hop — Varenna, Bellagio & Menaggio
  • Villa del Balbianello — the Star Wars & Bond villa, by boat
  • Swim & stroll — lakeside gardens, the Brunate funicular view
À table · Lake Como
  • Risotto al pesce persico — risotto crowned with butter-fried lake perch
  • Missoltini — sun-dried lake shad, grilled with polenta
  • Lavarello — pan-fried whitefish with sage & lemon
  • Polenta uncia — buttery polenta layered with cheese
  • Pizzoccheri — Valtellina buckwheat pasta with cabbage & cheese
IV
Chapter Four · The Lakes II · Jul 8–12

Lake Garda — the fun one

Italy's biggest lake, and its best for a nine-year-old. Theme-park thrills, a fairy-tale castle ringed by Roman ruins, lake swimming, and a 2,000-year-old arena a half-hour down the road in Verona.

Base in the south near Peschiera — train-connected and right by the rides — then split the days between water, history, and pure kid joy.

  • Gardaland — Italy's biggest theme park, a full day of rides
  • Sirmione — the Scaliger water-castle & Grotte di Catullo
  • Verona day trip — the Roman Arena & Juliet's balcony
  • Malcesine — cable car up Monte Baldo, boats on the lake
À table · Lake Garda
  • Bigoli con le sardelle — thick pasta with lake-sardine sauce
  • Carne salada — brine-cured beef, sliced thin with oil & lemon
  • Pesce in carpione — lake fish in a sweet-sour marinade
  • Garda DOP olive oil — Europe's northernmost, delicate & fruity
  • Gelato — lakeside, by the scoop, every evening
V
Finale · Milan & Home · Jul 12–13

One last night by the Duomo

A final northern-Italian evening beneath the cathedral's marble spires, then a daytime flight home — into Newark, the savviest way back and a short drive from Simsbury.

Rooftop views off the Duomo, a last gelato in the Galleria, and a night near the airport before the morning flight. You're home by late afternoon, a day inside the deadline.
