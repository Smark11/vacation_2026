I
Chapter One · Iceland · Jun 25–30

Begin where the sun won't set

A five-and-a-half-hour red-eye from Newark drops you into a land of erupting geysers, black-sand beaches, and waterfalls you can walk behind — beneath a sky that glows for twenty-one hours a day. Four days to soak in geothermal water, drive the Golden Circle, chase the South Coast, and let a nine-year-old's jaw stay open. Then one Icelandair hop carries you on to Milan.

Reykjavík & the Ring of Fire

Base for the four days is Reykjavík — small, walkable, and a 45-minute drive from Keflavík airport. Rent a 2WD or small SUV at the airport for the road trips on Days 2–3 (Iceland's paved Ring Road is easy in summer; the F-roads are not on this itinerary).

Iceland in practice

Currency
The króna (ISK)

Cards everywhere — even the smallest food truck. Skip the cash. Tipping isn't expected.

Tap water
The best in the world

Drink straight from any tap; refill bottles freely. Hot tap water can smell sulfury — that's normal (it's geothermal).

Daylight
~21 hours of sun

Late June is the Midnight Sun. Pack a sleep mask for the nine-year-old; expect the sky to glow at 1 AM.

Driving
2WD is fine in summer

Rent at KEF; the gravel + sand-and-ash insurance is worth it. Headlights on always. Sheep on roads. Check road.is.

Weather & packing
Cool & changeable

~50–55°F, but everything can happen in a day. Layers, waterproof shell and pants, sturdy shoes, swimsuits in the carry-on.

Safety
Sneaker waves & wind

Reynisfjara's waves are deadly — never turn your back. Download the 112 Iceland app. Don't open car doors in wind.

Four days in Iceland

Day 1 · Fri Jun 26 · Arrival

Soft landing & the Blue Lagoon

Off the overnight, pick up the car at KEF, and ease the jet lag in milky-warm geothermal water — the lagoon is right on the airport road. Then push into Reykjavík for an easy first afternoon. Stay up to local bedtime.

  • 9 AMBlue Lagoon — pre-book a time slot; "Comfort" package adds silica mud mask, a swim-up drink & towel
  • 1 PMReykjavík check-in — base in the 101 (downtown) neighbourhood
  • PMHallgrímskirkja + the rainbow Skólavörðustígur street + the Sun Voyager sculpture on the waterfront
  • EveBæjarins Beztu — a lamb hot dog "with everything" (since 1937)
Don't missFloating with a silica mud-mask on.
TipSwimsuit in your carry-on; leave conditioner in your hair before the lagoon.
Day 2 · Sat Jun 27 · Golden Circle

Geysers, rifts & a tomato greenhouse

Iceland's greatest hits in one ~230 km loop. Self-drive clockwise — total day ~7–8 hours with stops — and reserve lunch at the geothermal tomato farm in advance.

  • ~1hÞingvellir National Park — walk the Almannagjá rift between the North American & Eurasian plates
  • ~45mGeysir / Strokkur — Strokkur erupts ~every 5–10 minutes, 60+ feet skyward
  • ~45mGullfoss — the two-tier "Golden Falls"; upper and lower viewpoints
  • LunchFriðheimar — tomato soup in a working greenhouse (book ahead)
  • Add-onKerið volcanic crater or Secret Lagoon at Flúðir (calmer than Blue Lagoon)
Don't missStrokkur erupting — the kid wow moment.
TipSelf-drive beats the bus; pack snacks; reserve Friðheimar weeks out.
Day 3 · Sun Jun 28 · South Coast

Waterfalls, black sand & basalt

Long day — out by 8 AM along Ring Road 1, ~190 km each way. Bring full waterproofs (you will get soaked behind Seljalandsfoss) and a thermos. Dinner in the village of Vík breaks the drive home.

  • ~1.5h driveSeljalandsfoss — walk the path behind the falls; peek next door at hidden Gljúfrabúi
  • ~30m driveSkógafoss — 60 m curtain of water; 527 steps to the top viewpoint if energetic
  • DetourSólheimajökull glacier — short walk to a real glacier tongue
  • ⚠️ CareReynisfjara — basalt columns, sea stacks; deadly sneaker waves — keep well back
  • ~10m driveDyrhólaey — clifftop arch, summer puffins
  • EveDinner in Vík — Black Beach Restaurant or Halldórskaffi, then the drive back in summer twilight
Don't missWalking behind Seljalandsfoss; basalt at Reynisfjara.
Tip⚠️ Never turn your back to the surf at Reynisfjara — obey the warning lights.
Day 4 · Mon Jun 29 · Flex day

On the water, in the steam

An easier day after the long South Coast drive. Morning out on Faxaflói Bay; afternoon either inside the city or hiking up to a warm river you can actually sit in.

  • AM ~3hWhale watching from the Old Harbour — Elding or Special Tours; minkes, humpbacks, dolphins, summer puffins
  • PMPerlan — indoor ice cave + planetarium + viewing deck, or…
  • PMLaugardalslaug pool — 50 m outdoor pool, hot tubs & two waterslides, or…
  • ~6h round-tripReykjadalur "Hot River" hike — soak in a warm bathing river (a hidden gem)
  • EveLast Iceland dinner — Café Loki, Matur og Drykkur, or Grillmarkaðurinn
Don't missA humpback breach in Faxaflói Bay.
TipDress warmer than you'd think for the boat; bring motion-sickness tablets.

More of the island

II
Chapter Two · The Ligurian Coast · Jul 1–5

Five villages, one little train

The warm heart of the trip, and the one fixed point on the calendar. Monterosso al Mare — the only Cinque Terre village with a real sandy beach — is home base for four days of swimming, focaccia, and pesto where it was invented.

The five villages are stitched into the cliffs and linked by the Cinque Terre Express, a regional train that pops between tunnels every few minutes. Hop them at will, ride a boat past the headlands at golden hour, and take the gentle coastal paths between swims.

  • Village-hop — Riomaggiore, Manarola, Corniglia, Vernazza, Monterosso
  • Beach days — Monterosso's sand, sun, and seaside cafés
  • By boat — the whole coast from the water at sunset
Fixed datesHotel booked4 nights
À table · Liguria
  • Trofie al pesto — hand-rolled pasta twists with basil pesto. dal Billy, Manarola
  • Focaccia di Recco — molten-cheese-filled thin focaccia
  • Acciughe di Monterosso — prized anchovies, raw or fried
  • Farinata — crisp baked chickpea pancake
  • Frittura mista — a paper cone of fried seafood
III
Chapter Three · The Lakes I · Jul 5–8

Lake Como, impossibly elegant

Glacier-carved, villa-lined, and gorgeous from every angle. From a base in Varenna you ferry between the lake's prettiest towns, wander garden terraces, and swim off warm stone — somewhere brand new to all three of you.

Make Varenna your base — small, romantic, and on the train line — then let the ferries do the work. Bellagio and Menaggio are minutes across the water, and a boat ride away sits one of cinema's most famous villas.

  • Ferry-hop — Varenna, Bellagio & Menaggio
  • Villa del Balbianello — the Star Wars & Bond villa, by boat
  • Swim & stroll — lakeside gardens, the Brunate funicular view
À table · Lake Como
  • Risotto al pesce persico — risotto crowned with butter-fried lake perch
  • Missoltini — sun-dried lake shad, grilled with polenta
  • Lavarello — pan-fried whitefish with sage & lemon
  • Polenta uncia — buttery polenta layered with cheese
  • Pizzoccheri — Valtellina buckwheat pasta with cabbage & cheese
IV
Chapter Four · The Lakes II · Jul 8–12

Lake Garda — the fun one

Italy's biggest lake, and its best for a nine-year-old. Theme-park thrills, a fairy-tale castle ringed by Roman ruins, lake swimming, and a 2,000-year-old arena a half-hour down the road in Verona.

Base in the south near Peschiera — train-connected and right by the rides — then split the days between water, history, and pure kid joy.

  • Gardaland — Italy's biggest theme park, a full day of rides
  • Sirmione — the Scaliger water-castle & Grotte di Catullo
  • Verona day trip — the Roman Arena & Juliet's balcony
  • Malcesine — cable car up Monte Baldo, boats on the lake
À table · Lake Garda
  • Bigoli con le sardelle — thick pasta with lake-sardine sauce
  • Carne salada — brine-cured beef, sliced thin with oil & lemon
  • Pesce in carpione — lake fish in a sweet-sour marinade
  • Garda DOP olive oil — Europe's northernmost, delicate & fruity
  • Gelato — lakeside, by the scoop, every evening
V
Finale · Milan & Home · Jul 12–13

One last night by the Duomo

A final northern-Italian evening beneath the cathedral's marble spires, then a daytime flight home — into Newark, and a ~2.5 hr drive back to Simsbury.

Rooftop views off the Duomo, a last gelato in the Galleria, and a night near the airport before the morning flight. You're home by late afternoon, a day inside the deadline.
