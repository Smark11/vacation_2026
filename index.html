I
Chapter One · The Northern Opener

Begin where the sun won't set

The trip opens far to the north, in a place engineered to make a nine-year-old's jaw drop — and to feel like a different planet before the Italian heat. Two ways to do it. We're leaning hard toward the first.

★ Our pick

Reykjavík & the Ring of Fire

Land of fire, ice, and the midnight sun. A five-hour red-eye from Boston drops you into a world of erupting geysers, black-sand beaches, and waterfalls you can walk behind — all beneath a sky that glows for twenty-one hours a day. Three or four days here, then a single Icelandair hop carries you straight on to Milan, no second ticket needed.

  • The Blue & Sky Lagoons — milky-turquoise geothermal soaks, kids welcome
  • The Golden Circle — Þingvellir's rift, the Geysir, thundering Gullfoss
  • The South Coast — Skógafoss, Seljalandsfoss & Reynisfjara's black sand
  • Midnight sun — ~21 hours of daylight; bring the kid an eye-mask

or, the storybook alternative

Copenhagen, the easy city

The gentler option: seven hours overnight to a flat, bike-friendly capital of candy-coloured canals and the world's loveliest old amusement park. Warmer than Iceland, walkable end to end, and a pace a kid can keep — then a short hop down to Pisa lands you closest to the coast.

  • Tivoli Gardens — 1843 amusement park, magic after dark
  • Nyhavn & the canals — boat tours past the painted houses
  • Two wheels — the whole city, by bike, with the kid
  • Closest landing — CPH → Pisa, then a 1.5-hr train to Monterosso

II
Chapter Two · The Ligurian Coast · Jul 1–5

Five villages, one little train

The warm heart of the trip, and the one fixed point on the calendar. Monterosso al Mare — the only Cinque Terre village with a real sandy beach — is home base for four days of swimming, focaccia, and pesto where it was invented.

The five villages are stitched into the cliffs and linked by the Cinque Terre Express, a regional train that pops between tunnels every few minutes. Hop them at will, ride a boat past the headlands at golden hour, and take the gentle coastal paths between swims.

  • Village-hop — Riomaggiore, Manarola, Corniglia, Vernazza, Monterosso
  • Beach days — Monterosso's sand, sun, and seaside cafés
  • By boat — the whole coast from the water at sunset
  • Eat — trofie al pesto, fried anchovies, focaccia di Recco

III
Chapter Three · The Lakes I · Jul 5–8

Lake Como, impossibly elegant

Glacier-carved, villa-lined, and gorgeous from every angle. From a base in Varenna you ferry between the lake's prettiest towns, wander garden terraces, and swim off warm stone — somewhere brand new to all three of you.

Make Varenna your base — small, romantic, and on the train line — then let the ferries do the work. Bellagio and Menaggio are minutes across the water, and a boat ride away sits one of cinema's most famous villas.

  • Ferry-hop — Varenna, Bellagio & Menaggio across the lake
  • Villa del Balbianello — the Star Wars & James Bond villa, by boat
  • Swim & stroll — lakeside gardens and the Brunate funicular view
IV
Chapter Four · The Lakes II · Jul 8–12

Lake Garda — the fun one

Italy's biggest lake, and its best for a nine-year-old. Theme-park thrills, a fairy-tale castle ringed by Roman ruins, lake swimming, and a 2,000-year-old arena a half-hour down the road in Verona.

Base in the south near Peschiera — train-connected and right by the rides. Then split the days between water, history, and pure kid joy.

  • Gardaland — Italy's biggest theme park, a full day of rides
  • Sirmione — the Scaliger water-castle & Grotte di Catullo Roman ruins
  • Verona day trip — the Roman Arena & Juliet's balcony
  • Malcesine — cable car up Monte Baldo, boats on the lake
V
Finale · Milan & Home · Jul 12–13

One last night by the Duomo

A final northern-Italian evening beneath the cathedral's marble spires, then a daytime flight home — into Newark, the savviest way back and a short drive from Simsbury.

Rooftop views off the Duomo, a last gelato in the Galleria, and a night near the airport before the morning flight. You're home by late afternoon, a day inside the deadline.
